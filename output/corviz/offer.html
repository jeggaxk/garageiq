A quick word from Jack before you read on

Why Corviz exists

I started looking at UK garage data because I kept noticing the same thing. Owners who had been in the same location for 5, 6, 7 years, with a decent customer base, good reviews on Google, a professional setup — and still losing ground year on year. Not to bad service. To silence.

A customer brings their car in for three years running. Then one year, nothing. The garage doesn't notice straight away — there are cars to turn around, phones to answer. But that customer has gone somewhere else. Probably not because they had a bad experience. Because nobody sent a reminder.

Every garage management system has a reminder function. Most garage owners I've spoken to tried it once, got a batch of angry texts back from wrong numbers, and turned it off. The problem wasn't the idea of reminders. It was that the tool fired blind — no check on whether the MOT had already been renewed elsewhere, no validation on the numbers, no approval step before it went out.

That's what I built Corviz to fix. One thing only: a reminder engine that doesn't fire blind.

Every reminder is cross-checked against the DVLA MOT database before it goes out. If a customer has already renewed their MOT somewhere else since their last visit to you, we catch it before the reminder reaches their phone. If the MOT date in your system doesn't match what DVLA holds, you're shown the disagreement and decide what to do. Nothing fires without you seeing it first.

Why it costs £99 and not £9

The £99 is not software access. It's my time.

I personally review your customer list before anything goes out. I cross-check it against DVLA. I send you a walkthrough video tailored to your specific garage management system within 24 hours of you uploading your data. I'm on WhatsApp for the full 90 days. I write you a full review at day 30, day 60, and day 90 with the actual numbers from your dashboard.

You couldn't buy that as a managed service for £99 anywhere. The reason I can offer it at this price is that I'm doing it for 10 garages in the first cohort, not 1,000. That's the constraint.

The product itself — the automated reminders, the DVLA cross-check, the dashboard — that runs without me after the pilot. But the pilot is the part where I make sure it's set up correctly for your specific situation, your data, your customers. That's worth doing properly.

Why there are only 10 founding spots

I can only do this well for a small number of garages at once. Concierge means something. If I took 50 garages in the first cohort, I couldn't give each one a personal walkthrough video, a real WhatsApp relationship, and a written review at day 30, 60, and 90. I could send bulk emails and call it concierge. I'm not doing that.

The first 10 garages get a locked rate of £39 a month for life. That rate never rises for them, regardless of what the standard rate does. In exchange, they're getting something that doesn't exist yet at full scale — direct founder access for 90 days, with a guarantee on the outcome.

When those 10 are gone, they're gone. The founding member rate closes. Standard pricing is £79 a month.
