I ask because the guide is written for garages with 300 to 1,500 customers. If that's you, you'll get more out of it. If it's not, I'd rather tell you now than have you read something that doesn't apply.

Hit reply with a rough number and I'll send the guide straight back.

Jack
Founder, Corviz

P.S. If you'd rather just have the guide without the question, reply with "send it" and I'll send it straight over.

---

**Word count:** 104

**Why this works:** Asking a question before sending the guide creates a genuine two-way conversation. Inbox providers treat replied-to emails as trusted — this improves deliverability for every email that follows. It also tells Jack immediately which subscribers are the right fit.

**On reply:** Trigger the guide delivery automation (send the PDF link). Tag the subscriber with their customer count if captured. Jack should read replies personally.

**Do not:** Send the guide in the optin confirmation email. The reply-first step is the mechanism.

## Email 2 — What happened when the reminder fired

**Send:** Day 2
**Subject:** What happened when the reminder fired
**Preview text:** Three customers had already booked elsewhere.

---

Hi [First name],

The problem with most garage reminder tools isn't that the idea is wrong.

It's what happens when they fire blind.

A reminder goes out. Three customers reply saying they've already renewed their MOT elsewhere. Two numbers bounce. One reply is angry.

The owner turns it off. Never touches the reminder function again.

What the system didn't do: check whether the MOT had already been renewed. Check whether the number was still valid. Check whether the vehicle was even still on the road.

Corviz cross-checks every reminder against the DVLA MOT database before anything fires. If a customer has already renewed their MOT elsewhere since their last visit to you, we catch it before the message reaches their phone.

The guide has a full breakdown of how often this pattern shows up — and what it costs when it doesn't get caught.

Jack

P.S. Has something like this happened at your garage? Reply and tell me. I want to understand how common it is.

---

**Word count:** 162

**Notes:** No fabricated case studies or named garages. This describes a pattern, not a specific customer. The DVLA language is accurate and within the locked rules: cross-check applies to MOT dates only.

## Email 3 — The maths most garage owners don't run

**Send:** Day 4
**Subject:** The maths most garage owners don't run
**Preview text:** 8 missing customers a month. Run the numbers.

---

Hi [First name],

Take your April from last year. Pull up April this year's bookings.

How many names from last year are missing?

Most garage owners have never done this. When they do, the answer is usually 8 to 15 names missing — from one month.

Multiply by 12. Multiply by £85 per recovered car — that's a £55 MOT plus a £30 average advisory fix-on. You get somewhere between £8,000 and £15,000 that left quietly last year without anyone sending a reminder.

The guide walks through exactly where that money goes. Not in round numbers — in five specific patterns that show up on almost every customer list.

If you want a rough estimate for your garage specifically, reply with how many customers you have and which months are your busiest for MOTs. I'll work it out and send you a number.

Jack

P.S. The guide is in your inbox from my first email. If it didn't arrive, reply here and I'll resend it.

**Word count:** 164

**Notes:** The £85/car figure and 8–15 customers per month are from the locked strategy brief — do not change. The offer to calculate a personal estimate is genuine; Jack should respond and do this for every reply.
