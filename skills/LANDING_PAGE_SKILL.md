# LANDING_PAGE_SKILL.md
## Landing Page Design and Conversion — Agent Reference Guide

Frameworks extracted from three sources: "I Studied 1000 Landing Pages — Here's What Works in 2025", "Perfect Landing Page Design Explained in 5 Minutes", and "The $1 Million Landing Page." This skill governs how every page in a funnel is built — what goes on it, in what order, and why. Use alongside FUNNEL_SKILL.md which governs the overall funnel structure and sequence.

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

A landing page is a salesperson. It has one job: get the visitor to take one specific action. Everything on the page either moves them toward that action or it does not belong on the page.

The ad gets the click. The landing page does the selling. Never confuse the two jobs.

---

## THE 5-SECOND RULE

You have 5 seconds to hook someone before they leave. 60% of visitors never scroll past the above the fold section. 100% of visitors see it. Spend 80–90% of your design and copy effort on this one section before touching anything else.

If the above the fold does not work, nothing below it matters.

---

## PAGE STRUCTURE — SECTION BY SECTION

### Section 1: Above the Fold (Hero)

Everything visible before the user scrolls. Must contain all of the following:

**Headline**
- One hard-hitting benefit that answers: why should I care?
- Ties directly to the dream outcome of the exact customer
- Never explain who you are or what you do in the headline — explain what they get
- Never use jargon or vague language that requires processing
- Must pass the one Mississippi two Mississippi test — understood instantly
- Wrong: "Digital Imaging Technician Course"
- Right: "Get paid to travel the world working with high-profile creatives"
- Conversion rate increases of 80%+ have been seen from headline changes alone

**Subheadline**
- Explains how the headline promise is delivered
- Communicates the USP — what makes this different from every competitor
- Can specify who it is for: "for coaches who want to scale past £10K/month without paid ads"

**Visual**
- Shows the product in use or the experience of working with you
- Must build emotional connection — happy people using the product outperform product-only shots
- The human brain processes images 60,000 times faster than text
- For service businesses: a photo of the person builds trust and helps visitors imagine working with them

**Social Proof (above the fold)**
- At least two forms of social proof above the fold — do not save it all for below
- Options: review count, star rating, client logos with quotes, number of customers, years in business
- Featured-on logos alone are no longer trusted — always add a quote from the publication
- Reviews without photos and names are not believed — always include name, photo, and source
- The more credibility built upfront, the higher the conversion rate

**Call to Action Button**
- One button, one action, benefit-driven label
- Not "submit" or "click here" — name the outcome: "Get my free checklist", "Book my free call", "Start the quiz"
- Must visually stand out from everything else on the page

**FUD Reducers (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)**
- Placed directly underneath the CTA button
- Address the anxieties around taking action: no credit card required, cancel anytime, free to start, 30-day guarantee
- Conversion rate increases of 40–60% from this one addition alone

### Section 2: Features and Benefits

The headline made a big promise. This section delivers the proof of how.

**The critical distinction:**
- Features = what it is (technical specs, what is included)
- Benefits = what it does for them (what their life looks like after)

People do not buy features. They buy the transformation the feature enables.

- Wrong: "200 horsepower engine"
- Right: "Goes 0 to 60 in 3.1 seconds"
- Wrong: "Weekly coaching calls included"
- Right: "Never get stuck alone — expert guidance every step of the way"

For every feature, ask: so what does that mean for the customer? The answer is the benefit.

---

### Section 3: Social Proof (Extended)

Reinforce the decision they are moving toward with evidence from people like them.

**What works:**
- Testimonials with full name, photo, and source — verifiable and believable
- Case studies with specific numbers — "went from 0 to £12K/month in 90 days"
- Video testimonials — highest trust format
- Before and after results
- Logos of recognisable clients or publications with an accompanying quote

**What does not work:**
- Anonymous reviews with no photo or name
- Repeated logos on a carousel
- Generic statements with no specifics — "this product changed my life"
- Featured-on logos with no accompanying quote

Social proof is everywhere now. Visitors are sceptical. Make yours verifiable and specific or it works against you.

---

### Section 4: Problem Agitate Solve (PAS)

For pages targeting cold traffic — people who have the problem but do not yet know or trust you.

**Structure:**
1. Call out the problem explicitly — name the specific frustration in their own language
2. Agitate it — make them feel the cost of not solving it, the pain of staying where they are
3. Present the solution — position your offer as the clear and obvious way out

This section creates the emotional delta that drives action. When someone feels the gap between where they are and where they could be, they act.

The problem agitate solve framework alone has produced conversion rate lifts of 282% when added to pages that previously only had logical appeals.

---

### Section 5: FAQ — Objection Handling

A landing page cannot answer questions in real time. The FAQ does the job of the live salesperson.

**How to build the FAQ:**
- List every reason someone would not buy
- Turn each objection into a question
- Answer it directly and specifically

Common objections to address: is this right for me, what if it does not work, how long does it take, what is the commitment, can I cancel, is this a scam, what makes you different.

The FAQ is not a nice-to-have. It is the section that handles the final 20% of visitors who are interested but not yet convinced.

---

### Section 6: Final Call to Action

Never let a visitor reach the bottom of the page without being told what to do next. Repeat the primary CTA at the end of every page.

- Same button, same benefit-driven label as above the fold
- Short reinforcing statement above it: "Join 5,000 people who have already started"
- FUD reducers again underneath

---

### Section 7: Footer

Organisational element only. Links to other pages, social media, legal pages. Not a conversion section — keep it minimal.

## THE SCANNING PRINCIPLE — DESIGN FOR SCANNERS NOT READERS

People read 20% of page content. They scan 80%. Design for the scanner, not the reader.

**Rules for scannable pages:**
- Every section headline must communicate its full benefit on its own — assume the visitor reads only the headlines
- Wrong section headline: "What makes us unique"
- Right section headline: "The only platform that builds your funnel and emails in one click"
- Use bullet points for value props — not paragraphs
- Bold the most important words and phrases
- Never hide key content in carousels, dropdowns, or accordions on mobile — what is hidden will not be seen
- Expose reviews in full on mobile — never put them in a carousel

**The headline-only test:**
Read only the headlines of your page in sequence. Does the full story of what you offer and why it matters come through? If not, rewrite the headlines until it does.

## VISUAL HIERARCHY

The art of guiding the visitor's eye from the most important element to the least important.

**The hierarchy order:**
1. Headline — largest, most prominent, highest contrast
2. Subheadline — clearly secondary, smaller and lighter
3. CTA button — visually distinct, contrasting colour, impossible to miss
4. Supporting copy — body text, standard size
5. Social proof — present but not competing with the CTA

**What kills visual hierarchy:**
- Multiple elements competing for attention at the same size and weight
- Too many CTA buttons — one action per page
- Colour clashes that make nothing stand out
- Body text the same size as headlines

**The less is more rule:**
When you give people too many choices they make no choice. Too many buttons, too many links, too many options creates analysis paralysis. One page, one action. Remove everything that does not serve that one action.

A 64% conversion rate lift has been seen from fixing visual hierarchy alone on pages where everything was competing for attention simultaneously.

## PAGE LENGTH

Long pages do not kill conversions. Bad structure does.

A page should be as long as it needs to be to answer every question and overcome every objection a cold visitor has before they are ready to act. For cold paid traffic this is usually longer than most people expect.

**The rule:**
Every section must earn its place by moving the visitor one step closer to the CTA. If a section does not do that, cut it.

Never hide content on mobile. Never use carousels for reviews. Expose everything.

---

## SIMPLICITY BEATS CREATIVITY

Jacob's Law: people expect a landing page to look and function like the other pages they already use. Deviation from familiar patterns creates friction and kills conversions.

**What this means in practice:**
- One headline, not two
- CTA button where people expect to find it — below the headline
- Social proof at the top — not buried halfway down
- Standard navigation patterns
- No unconventional layouts that make people think

Creativity is for the copy and the offer. Not for the layout. If your creative choices make a visitor pause to figure out what they are looking at, you have lost them.

## THE QUIZ AND SCORECARD FUNNEL — ADVANCED LEAD MAGNET FORMAT

An alternative to the standard PDF optin that produces higher quality leads and more qualification data. Best used when the target customer has a specific measurable problem.

**How it works:**
Visitor lands on a quiz page → answers 15 questions → receives a personalised dynamic results page → gets a recommended next step based on their score.

**The quiz landing page structure:**
- Hook: either a frustration hook ("Feeling frustrated that X happens even though you do Y") or a results hook ("Are you ready to Z?")
- Subheadline: "Answer 15 questions to find out why you're experiencing this and what to do about it"
- Value proposition: "We will measure and improve these three specific things" — list the three areas
- Credibility: who created this, their background, relevant research or statistics
- CTA: "Start the quiz — takes 3 minutes, completely free, get immediate recommendations"

Done correctly this produces 20–40% of visitors starting the quiz.

**The 15 questions:**

Questions 1–2: Name and email (mandatory). Phone number (optional). Location captured automatically.

Questions 3–12 (10 questions): Best practices questions — are they doing the 10 things they should be doing? Yes/no answers. These generate the score.

Questions 13–17 (5 qualifying questions):
1. Which best describes your current situation? (multiple choice — segments by experience level)
2. Which describes your desired outcome in the next 90 days? (reveals the primary motivation)
3. What is the obstacle stopping you or what have you tried that has not worked? (reveals objections)
4. Which solution would suit you best? (education, coaching, done-for-you — reveals budget range)
5. Is there anything else we need to know? (open text — people often reveal exactly what will close the sale)

**The dynamic results page:**
- Big reveal: overall score with a visual indicator (speedometer, thermometer, percentage)
- Three insights based on their answers
- Personalised next step based on score:
  - High score / highly qualified → offer a one-to-one call
  - Mid score → offer a group event or webinar
  - Low score / wrong fit → send to free content (video, podcast, book)

**Why this works:**
Every lead comes with a full qualification profile. You know their situation, their desired outcome, their obstacle, their budget range, and their own words about what they need. The follow-up conversation starts from a position of knowing the customer — not from cold outreach.

## EMOTIONAL VS LOGICAL CONVERSION

People buy with emotion and justify with logic. Both must be present on the page but emotion comes first.

**Emotional levers:**
- Images of happy people experiencing the outcome
- Copy that names the exact frustration in the customer's own language
- The problem agitate solve framework
- Aspirational language that lets them imagine the result

**Logical levers:**
- Specific numbers and results
- Verifiable social proof
- Clear feature and benefit explanations
- FAQ objection handling
- Guarantees and risk reversals

The winning formula: lead with emotion to get them engaged, back it up with logic to get them over the line.

## TWO HOOK FRAMEWORKS FOR HEADLINES

**The frustration hook:**
"Feeling frustrated that [problem] even though you [effort being made]"
- "Feeling frustrated that you're not getting investment returns even though you invest consistently every month"
- Forces immediate identification — if the frustration is real, they feel seen

**The results/readiness hook:**
"Are you ready to [desired outcome]?"
- "Are you ready to run your first marathon?"
- Creates curiosity and self-assessment — makes them want to find out if they qualify

## AGENT OPERATING PRINCIPLES

When building or reviewing any landing page, the agent must:

1. **Start with the above the fold** — spend the majority of effort here before anything else
2. **Apply the 5-second rule** — if the headline does not hook in 5 seconds, rewrite it before touching anything else
3. **Name the outcome not the method** — every headline must state what the visitor gets, not what you do
4. **Always include social proof above the fold** — at least two forms, both specific and verifiable
5. **Reduce FUDs directly under every CTA** — fear, uncertainty, and doubt kill conversions at the point of action
6. **Design for scanners** — every section headline must tell the story on its own
7. **Enforce visual hierarchy** — one dominant element per section, one CTA per page
8. **Apply simplicity over creativity** — familiar layouts convert better than clever ones
9. **Use PAS for cold traffic pages** — problem agitate solve before presenting the solution
10. **Include FAQ** — every objection must be answered before the final CTA
11. **Consider the quiz format** — when the target customer has a specific measurable problem, a scorecard funnel produces higher quality leads than a standard PDF optin
12. **Test emotional connection first** — images and copy that create emotional resonance before logical proof
