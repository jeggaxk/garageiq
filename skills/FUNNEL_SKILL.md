# FUNNEL_SKILL.md
## Funnel Building Frameworks — Agent Reference Guide

Frameworks extracted from "The Most Valuable Funnel Training You'll Ever Watch" and "37 Money-Making Secrets to Instantly Make Your Ads More Profitable." This skill governs what happens after the ad is clicked — the page structure, the offer sequence, the psychology of conversion, and the order in which to build and test everything. Use after OFFER_SKILL.md has defined what is being sold and MARKETING_SKILL.md has defined who is being targeted.

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

A simple funnel that works beats a complex funnel that does not. The goal is not an impressive funnel — it is a reliable one that performs in most conditions without requiring advanced skill to maintain. Build the equivalent of a beginner surfboard: not the fastest, not the flashiest, but the most fun to ride and suitable for 80–90% of conditions.

Three pages. That is the whole funnel.

---

## THE THREE-PAGE SURFBOARD FUNNEL

### Page 1 — The Optin Page
**Purpose:** capture the lead (name, email, phone number)

**Structure:**
- One clear headline that states the specific outcome the lead magnet delivers
- Three bullet points maximum — what they will get, why it matters, how fast
- A button (not a form) — clicking the button opens a two-part optin pop-up
- The pop-up collects name, email, and phone number

**Why a button instead of a visible form:**
Clicking a button creates micro-commitment. The person has taken a small action and is therefore more likely to complete the form than if the form were visible immediately. Skin in the game psychology.

**On asking for phone numbers:**
Most marketers avoid this out of fear. Test it. The reason people do not give their phone number is not that they object to it — it is that the offer is not compelling enough. A strong enough offer will get the phone number. Phone numbers dramatically increase follow-up conversion rates.

**Optin page copy rule:**
The headline must pass the one Mississippi two Mississippi test — the reader must understand immediately what they are getting and why it matters. Name the outcome, not the method.

- Wrong: "Access our Apex Campaigns system"
- Right: "$25,000 clients — how to find, sell, close, and deliver them"

Nobody knows what Apex Campaigns is. Everyone knows what $25,000 clients means.

**What NOT to do after optin:**
- Do not leave them on the same page with a thank you message
- Do not redirect them straight to the lead magnet document
Both of these kill the conversion opportunity at peak interest.

**What TO do:**
Redirect immediately to page 2.

---

### Page 2 — The Offer Page
**Purpose:** convert the lead into a buyer, a booked call, or a qualified prospect

**The psychology of immediate offers:**
A person's interest and likelihood to buy is at its absolute peak the moment they opt in. They have just demonstrated they have the problem. They have just taken an action. This is the highest point on the interest curve — it will only decline from here. The correct time to make the next offer is immediately, not after a nurturing sequence.

**Nurturing is a mistake.** Waiting days or weeks to make an offer after optin means trying to re-engage someone whose interest has already dropped significantly. Strike while the iron is hot.

**Page 2 format — the Video Sales Letter (VSL):**
- Position the video as training, not as a sales pitch — "watch this free training on how to [achieve the outcome]"
- The video presents the problem, agitates it, introduces the solution, and makes the offer
- Below the video: a single clear CTA button

**What to offer on page 2:**
Three options depending on business model — test to find what works:
1. A booked call (free 10-minute discovery/qualification call) — works best for high ticket
2. A low ticket product (£7–£97 book, PDF, mini course) — works best for building a buyer list
3. A mid ticket product (£297–£997 course or programme) — works best when trust is already established via the lead magnet

**Conversion benchmarks:**
- Purchase conversion: 1–2% of optins
- Call booking conversion: 3–5% of optins
- If conversion is higher than these — test a higher price point
- If conversion is lower — the offer or the copy is the problem, not the traffic

### Page 3 — The One-Click Upsell
**Purpose:** build profit and increase average order value immediately after a purchase or booking

**The logic:**
The hardest part is getting a customer. Once someone has bought or booked, they have already overcome the biggest psychological barrier — the decision to trust you with money or time. The cost of acquiring the next purchase from this person is near zero. This is where profit is built.

**Structure:**
- Immediate redirect after purchase or booking confirmation
- Single offer — do not present multiple options
- Positioned as a natural next step: if page 2 sold the what, page 3 sells the how or the faster/deeper version
- One-click purchase — do not make them re-enter payment details

**Upsell ladder example:**
- Optin: free checklist on how to add £10K/month
- Page 2: £7 book — comprehensive A-to-Z on getting to £10K/month
- Page 3: £500 programme — six-week training with worksheets and hand-holding

Each step is the same promise delivered faster and with more support. The mantra throughout: faster and easier.

## PRE-LANDERS — THE PAGE BEFORE THE PAGE

A pre-lander is a page that sits between the ad and the main offer page. Its job is to warm the prospect and disguise the sales process so they do not feel like they are being sold to before they even arrive at the offer.

**Two proven pre-lander formats:**

**Advertorial**
Looks like an editorial article or blog post written by a third party. Tells a story, presents a problem, introduces the solution naturally. Never looks like it was written by the brand. The reader consumes it as content, not as advertising. By the time they reach the CTA they have already been sold without realising it.

**Listicle**
A list-format pre-lander — five reasons why, five gifts for this season, top three solutions for this problem. Same principle as the advertorial — looks like editorial content, not a brand promotion. Works especially well for products where comparison or education is part of the buying decision.

**When to use a pre-lander:**
When cold traffic is not converting directly from ad to offer page. When the product requires more context or education before the ask. When the market sophistication level is high and a direct offer feels too abrupt.

**The rule:**
A pre-lander must not look like it was made by the brand. The moment it looks promotional it loses its entire conversion advantage.

## QUIZ FUNNELS — MICRO-COMMITMENT ACQUISITION

The quiz funnel is currently one of the highest converting acquisition models for large brands because it generates micro-commitments from the prospect before they ever reach the offer.

Every question answered is a small act of investment. By the time they reach the results page they have already committed time, revealed their situation, and received personalised value. The psychological barrier to buying is significantly lower than from a cold optin.

**When to default to a quiz funnel over a standard optin:**
- The product requires some personalisation or matching to the customer's specific situation
- The market is sophisticated and a standard lead magnet feels generic
- The goal is higher quality leads with pre-qualification data built in
- The business sells at a premium price point where lead quality matters more than lead volume

Refer to LANDING_PAGE_SKILL.md for the full quiz funnel structure and the 15-question framework.

## THE COPY PRINCIPLE THAT DRIVES EVERYTHING

**Name the outcome, not the method or the brand.**

Every page, every headline, every offer name must answer: what specific result does the customer get, in what timeframe?

- Wrong: Apex Campaigns
- Right: $25,000 Clients
- Wrong: Our 6-week programme
- Right: Lose 5kg in 21 days
- Wrong: Complete hair loss solution
- Right: Regrow your hairline in 90 days — free consultation

The offer IS the outcome. Strip away everything that describes what you do and replace it with what they get. If someone cannot read the headline and immediately understand the specific result being promised, rewrite it.

**The currency of the offer:**
Identify the one number your customer wants to see go up or down in a specific amount of time. Build the entire offer around that one number. Everything else is noise.

## THE BUILD SEQUENCE — DO NOT BUILD UNTIL YOU HAVE A CONVERSION

The most common and expensive funnel mistake: building everything before testing whether anyone wants it.

**The correct sequence:**

1. **Define the offer** — outcome, timeframe, who it is for. One sentence.
2. **Find communities** — forums, Facebook groups, LinkedIn, YouTube comment sections, Reddit, podcasts. Wherever your exact customer already congregates.
3. **Post the offer as text** — no funnel, no product, no lead magnet yet. Just post the offer and see if people engage. If they do not engage, the offer is wrong — fix it before building anything.
4. **Redirect engagers to a minimal optin page** — headline, three bullets, button. Nothing else. Collect names and emails.
5. **If you get optins but have no lead magnet yet** — that is fine. Send a personal email apologising for a technical issue with the delivery and handle it manually. You are testing demand, not delivering a product.
6. **When optins convert to calls or sales** — now you have proof. Build the lead magnet. Build the product. Build the full funnel.
7. **When the funnel is profitable organically** — now add paid ads.

**Do not create anything until you have a conversion.** Not the lead magnet, not the product, not the course. Test first. Build second.

**Ads only scale what is already working.** Ads are fuel. Do not add fuel to a fire that does not exist yet. Pouring ad spend into an unproven offer does not fix the offer — it just makes the failure more expensive, faster.

## TRAFFIC — ORGANIC BEFORE PAID

**Before spending on ads:**
- Find 5–10 communities where your exact customer already exists
- Add genuine value in those communities — answer questions, share insights, become recognisable
- Post the offer as content — not as a pitch, as a result or a transformation
- Build a small following or email list organically first

**When to switch to paid ads:**
- You have proven the offer converts organically
- You have a positive unit economics baseline — you know roughly what a lead costs and what it converts to
- You have a product or service that can be delivered at scale
- You have the margin to afford paid acquisition

## FOLLOW-UP — EMAIL AND PHONE

Everyone who opts in and does not buy immediately goes into follow-up. The sequence is short and direct — not a nurture sequence.

**Email follow-up:**
- Email 1 (immediate): deliver the lead magnet and make the offer again
- Email 2 (day 2): address the most common objection
- Email 3 (day 3): social proof or case study
- Email 4 (day 5): urgency or close

**Phone follow-up:**
If you have their phone number, call or text within 24 hours. Introduce yourself, reference the lead magnet they downloaded, and ask one qualifying question. Do not pitch on the first contact — qualify first, pitch second.

## THE QUALIFICATION CALL

For high-ticket offers, the goal of the funnel is a booked call, not a direct sale. The call is where the sale happens.

**The 10-minute discovery call structure:**
- Confirm they have the problem your offer solves
- Understand where they are now and where they want to be
- Establish the cost of not solving it
- Present the offer as the bridge
- Ask for the sale or a next step

Position the call as a free roadblock remover or diagnostic — not a sales call. People are far more likely to book a call that promises to solve a specific problem than one that feels like a pitch.

## FUNNEL DIAGNOSTICS — WHY FUNNELS FAIL

If the funnel is not converting, work through these in order:

**Problem 1: Low optin rate**
- Headline is not clear enough — does not name a specific outcome
- The lead magnet is not compelling enough for the target customer
- The page has too much on it — simplify

**Problem 2: Low offer page conversion**
- The offer does not feel like a natural next step from the lead magnet
- The VSL is too long or does not get to the point fast enough
- The CTA is unclear — one button, one action, one outcome

**Problem 3: Low upsell take rate**
- The upsell is not positioned as faster or easier — it feels like more work, not less
- The price jump is too large relative to the page 2 offer
- The page asks them to re-enter payment details — remove that friction

**Problem 4: Ads converting but funnel not converting**
- Check that the ad promise matches the optin page promise exactly — any disconnect kills conversion
- Organic traffic that converted does not mean paid traffic will behave the same way — paid traffic is colder and needs more clarity upfront

**The root cause of most funnel failures:**
The offer name or headline describes what you do, not what the customer gets. Fix the copy before fixing the funnel.

## AGENT OPERATING PRINCIPLES

When building or reviewing any funnel, the agent must:

1. **Start with the offer name** — does it name the specific outcome and timeframe? If not, fix it before anything else
2. **Default to three pages** — optin, offer, upsell. Do not add complexity until simplicity has been proven
3. **Never recommend nurturing before an immediate offer** — make the offer at peak interest, immediately after optin
4. **Always include an upsell** — this is where profit is built, not on the front end
5. **Enforce the build sequence** — define offer, test in communities, get optins, get conversions, then build the product
6. **Never recommend paid ads on an unproven offer** — organic proof first, paid scale second
7. **Diagnose funnel failures in order** — optin rate first, offer page second, upsell third, ad-to-page match fourth
8. **Apply the currency principle** — identify the one number the customer wants to move and build the entire funnel around that number
9. **Consider a pre-lander for cold traffic** — when direct ad to offer page is not converting, add an advertorial or listicle before the optin page
10. **Default to a quiz funnel for premium offers** — when lead quality matters more than lead volume, the quiz funnel outperforms a standard optin
