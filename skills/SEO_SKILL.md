## THE SEARCH EVERYWHERE REALITY

Google handles approximately 13.7 billion searches per day — but that is only 27% of all search activity on the internet. The other 73% is happening across TikTok, Instagram, Reddit, YouTube, Amazon, ChatGPT, Claude, podcasts, and app stores.

Customers no longer follow a linear funnel. They follow a constellation of micro-decisions:
- What to click — Google
- What to trust — Reddit threads and reviews
- What to buy — Amazon, TikTok Shop
- What to think — YouTube videos and podcasts
- What to believe — ChatGPT, Claude, AI tools
- Who to follow — Instagram, LinkedIn

**The implication:** optimising only for Google while customers are deciding on Reddit, validating on Amazon, and asking ChatGPT for recommendations means being invisible at the actual moment of decision — even with a strong Google ranking.

**Search Everywhere Optimisation** is not about being on every platform simultaneously. It is about strategic presence on the specific platforms where your exact customer makes buying decisions. Use the RICE framework to prioritise:
- R — Reach: how many people search on this platform daily
- I — Impact: how much business impact could this platform have
- C — Confidence: how confident are you that you can succeed here
- E — Ease: how easy is it for you to execute

Score each 1–10, multiply by reach, and focus on the top two to three platforms first.

## PART 1: TRADITIONAL SEO FOUNDATIONS

Strong traditional SEO is the foundation of everything — including AI search. You will not show up in AI responses if you do not first rank well organically. These are not competing disciplines. AI search is an additional layer built on top of traditional SEO done well.

### Step 1 — Identify Your Most Valuable Keywords

Start with business-driving keywords — the exact terms your best customers would type when looking for what you sell. In priority order:

1. Your brand name
2. Explicit names of your products or services
3. Keywords describing problems your product solves
4. Local modifiers if relevant (city, region)
5. Competitor alternatives (yes — rank for "[Competitor] alternatives")

**Search intent matching — the most important SEO decision:**
For every target keyword, Google the term and study what appears. Google is showing you what it believes people actually want when they type that phrase. Your page must match that intent — not just the topic.

Four types of search intent:
- Informational — people want to learn something (blog posts, guides, FAQs)
- Navigational — people want to find a specific website or brand
- Commercial — people are researching before buying (comparison posts, reviews, roundups)
- Transactional — people are ready to buy (product pages, service pages)

If the top results for your keyword are all roundup comparison articles and you are trying to rank a product page — you will not rank. Match the format the intent demands.

**The backdoor approach for smaller brands:**
If dominant brands own the homepage rankings for a keyword, do not compete head-on. Create a roundup or comparison article that provides genuine value — even mentioning competitors — and earn rankings through content authority rather than brand authority. This works consistently even against brands with far greater domain authority.

### Step 2 — Build the Best Possible Page

**Content completeness beats word count:**
Google does not reward a specific word count. The question is not how long should this be — it is what does someone searching this term need to know, and have I covered it completely. Write to cover the topic fully, then stop.

**The E-E-A-T framework — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness:**
This is what Google uses to evaluate content quality and what AI tools use to decide whether to cite a source.

Experience — demonstrate first-hand knowledge. Share specific case studies, real results, personal observations. AI is increasingly good at detecting surface-level content versus genuine expertise.

Expertise — include author credentials and bios. Cite reputable sources. Present multiple perspectives on complex topics. Address objections and limitations honestly.

Authoritativeness — be mentioned and cited across the web. Quality backlinks, press mentions, inclusion in best-of lists, citations in other articles.

Trustworthiness — accurate information, transparent sourcing, clear who wrote it and why they are qualified, consistent brand presence across platforms.

**Psychographic audience mapping over demographic targeting:**
Do not write to a demographic (women aged 30–40). Write to a psychographic profile — a specific mindset, set of fears, motivations, and behaviours. Map multiple customer profiles for the same product and create content that speaks directly to each one. This is increasingly important as AI tools deliver personalised results based on where the user is in their journey.

### Step 3 — Power Up Your Pages

**Topical authority — the compounding SEO strategy:**
One great page on a topic will not rank as well as one great page supported by an entire ecosystem of related content. Build content clusters:

- Money page (the page you most want to rank) — comprehensive, high-quality, targets your primary keyword
- Supporting articles — related topics, subtopics, questions your audience asks, each internally linking back to the money page
- Pillar content — the most comprehensive single resource on the topic, acts as the hub of the cluster

A website with 20 interrelated articles on hydroponic gardening will outrank a website with one perfect article on the same topic — even if that one article is better written.

**Internal linking — the mechanism that passes authority:**
Every supporting article should link back to its money page. When a supporting article earns a backlink from an external site, that authority flows through the internal link to the money page. This is how small brands build authority without needing backlinks directly to their most important pages.

**Backlinks — build content worth linking to first:**
The best backlink strategy is creating content so useful that sites naturally link to it. Second best is outreach — finding sites that have linked to outdated resources and pitching your more current, more useful version as a replacement. Never pitch a sales page for backlinks. Pitch genuinely valuable content.

**Content-driven commerce — embed conversion in content:**
Do not put a CTA only at the bottom of content pages. Embed relevant product links, inquiry forms, and conversion actions naturally within the content at the point of highest relevance. When someone reads about a topic and a relevant product or next step appears in context — conversion rates increase significantly. This applies to both e-commerce (product links in blog posts) and service businesses (inquiry forms embedded mid-article).

**Seasonality — plan content before the search spike:**
Most businesses have seasonal search patterns even if they do not realise it. Identify when your audience searches for specific topics across the year and publish or refresh content in the weeks before those peaks — not during them. Content published just before a topic starts trending gets indexed and gains authority before the competition, which is what enables Google Discover visibility during the peak.

## PART 2: AI SEARCH OPTIMISATION

AI search is not replacing traditional SEO — it is an additional layer. The same foundations apply, with specific structural choices that make content easier for AI tools to parse and cite.

### How AI Tools Select Sources

AI models do not scroll through results the way humans do. They summarise — pulling from sources that are mentioned most frequently across the web and that demonstrate clear trustworthiness signals. Being cited by an AI tool is not about gaming an algorithm. It is about being a genuinely authoritative, well-structured, widely-referenced source.

The metric that matters for AI visibility is not click-through rate — it is brand mentions. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity a question in your space, does your brand appear in the answer? That is the goal.

### Writing Content AI Loves

**Natural language question-based structure:**
Use H2 and H3 headings framed as complete natural language questions — the kind a real person would type or speak. Then answer directly and conversationally underneath. AI models are built on conversational text and naturally prefer this format.

- Wrong heading: "Running shoes — ranked"
- Right heading: "What are the best running shoes for marathon training?"

**Semantic richness:**
Rather than repeating the same keyword, use a natural variety of related terms, concepts, and entities that create a web of meaning around your topic. AI tools understand context and topic relationships — the more completely you cover a topic's semantic territory, the more authoritative you appear.

**Structured formats AI can quote directly:**
- FAQ sections — short specific questions with direct one-sentence answers. AI tools quote these verbatim.
- Bullet point lists — for steps, tips, and collections of items. Easy for AI to extract and present.
- Tables — for comparisons, features, specs, pricing. AI can parse and cite specific cells.
- Numbered lists — for sequences and processes.

What is hidden in paragraphs gets missed. What is structured in lists and tables gets cited.

**The FAQ format for direct AI citation:**
Write specific questions as they would actually be asked, then answer immediately and completely.
Example: "Does [product] integrate with Salesforce? Yes, [product] offers a native Salesforce integration that syncs contact data in real time."
This is the exact format AI tools pull from to answer user questions.

### Technical Considerations for AI Crawlability

**Robots.txt — do not block AI crawlers:**
Check your robots.txt file and ensure you are not blocking OpenAI's GPTBot, Google's crawlers, or Microsoft's Bingbot. AI tools cannot index what they cannot crawl.

**HTML-first content:**
AI systems primarily read raw HTML. Key information that only appears after JavaScript interactions will be missed entirely. Put your most important content in the raw HTML, not in JavaScript-rendered elements.

**Alt text and transcripts:**
Always include descriptive alt text on images and transcripts for videos. AI cannot see images or hear audio — these are the only ways to make that content accessible to AI indexing.

**Site speed and structure:**
Fast loading, logical hierarchy, most valuable content within a few clicks of the homepage. These are basic SEO principles that also directly affect AI crawlability.

### Schema Markup — Tell AI Exactly What Your Page Is

Schema markup is code that humans cannot see but machines use to understand precisely what type of information a page contains. It increases the likelihood of being featured in AI answers and rich search results significantly.

**Priority schema types:**
- Organisation schema — your brand name, logo, social profiles, founding date. Establishes your entity in knowledge graphs that AI systems reference.
- Article schema — publication date, author, headline. Helps AI determine relevance and credibility of content.
- FAQ schema — marks up question and answer pairs so AI tools can extract and present them directly.
- Product schema — for e-commerce, signals price, availability, and reviews to both Google and AI tools.
- Local business schema — for location-based businesses, establishes physical presence and service area.

Schema does not guarantee AI citation but significantly increases the probability by removing all ambiguity about what the page contains and who produced it.

### Building Authority Across the AI Knowledge Graph

AI tools do not evaluate your content in isolation. They consider what the rest of the web says about you. The same trust signals that build Google authority build AI visibility:

**Cross-platform presence:**
Ensure your brand is listed and consistent across Google My Business, Bing Places, Yelp, LinkedIn company page, Apple Maps, Crunchbase, and any industry-specific databases. AI tools sample these sources when compiling answers about businesses.

**Wikipedia and Wikidata:**
Even without a full Wikipedia article, adding your business to Wikidata (the structured database behind Wikipedia) puts your entity into one of the primary sources AI models are trained on and reference.

**Earned media and citations:**
Proactively pitch to industry roundup articles, best-of lists, and publications in your space. Being listed in "Top 10 [your category]" articles on respected sites is gold — AI answering "What are the best [your category]?" will reference those lists.

**Original data and proprietary research:**
Conduct surveys. Publish original statistics about your industry. Create tools and calculators. These become citable assets that journalists, bloggers, and AI tools reference repeatedly — often for years after publication. First-party data that quantifies a problem in your industry will always be useful and always be cited.

**Thought leadership and named expertise:**
Attribute content to named authors with credentials and backstories. A page signed by "Sarah Chen, 15 years in compliance law" carries more E-E-A-T weight than unsigned content. Build profiles for your key contributors across the web so their expertise is verifiable and connected to your domain.

## PART 3: PLATFORM-SPECIFIC DECISION OPTIMISATION

Each platform where your customers make decisions has its own psychology and its own content code. The same content cannot be copy-pasted across platforms.

**Google** — depth, authority, and search intent matching. People come to learn and evaluate.

**TikTok** — emotion and novelty. Decisions are immediate and feeling-driven. Content must be visual and emotionally resonant within the first second.

**YouTube** — retention and perceived expertise. People invest time expecting depth. Authority must be demonstrated, not claimed.

**ChatGPT and Claude** — semantic clarity and citations. AI tools want clear factual information from authoritative sources. No emotional hooks, no visual flourishes — just structured, accurate, well-sourced content.

**Amazon** — social proof and trust. Customers go straight to reviews. Review quality, quantity, and recency are the only signals that matter.

**Reddit** — raw authenticity. Any hint of marketing language destroys credibility instantly. Genuine participation in communities, honest answers, and value-first engagement are the only approaches that work.

**Instagram** — aspirational identity. People are buying a version of themselves, not a product. Lifestyle, aspiration, and identity alignment drive decisions.

## PART 4: INTEGRATION WITH THE BUILD PROCESS

When the web agent builds any page, apply SEO principles from the first line of code — not as an afterthought after the page is built.

**Every page must have:**
- A title tag that includes the primary keyword naturally and matches search intent
- A meta description under 160 characters that acts as ad copy — states the specific outcome and includes a CTA
- One H1 that matches the page's primary keyword intent
- H2 and H3 structured as natural language questions where appropriate
- Alt text on every image — descriptive, specific, includes the keyword naturally where relevant
- Internal links to and from related pages
- Schema markup appropriate to the page type
- Fast load time — no unnecessary scripts, compressed images, minimal dependencies
- Mobile-first responsive design

**Content pages additionally need:**
- FAQ section structured for AI citation
- Author attribution with credentials where relevant
- Cited sources and statistics with links
- Related content links building toward topical authority
- Embedded CTAs at points of highest relevance in the content — not only at the bottom

**The money page hierarchy:**
Every site should have a clear hierarchy with money pages (the pages that generate leads or sales) at the centre, supported by clusters of related content pages all internally linking back to the money pages. Build this structure into the site architecture from day one — retrofitting it later is significantly harder.

## AGENT OPERATING PRINCIPLES

When building any page or advising on content strategy with an SEO component, the agent must:

1. **Match search intent before writing a word** — identify what format and content type the search intent demands and match it precisely
2. **Apply E-E-A-T to every content page** — every piece must demonstrate experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness through specific signals not general claims
3. **Structure for AI citation** — use natural language question headings, FAQ sections, bullet points, and tables on every content page
4. **Add schema markup to every page** — minimum organisation and article schema, with additional types appropriate to the page content
5. **Never block AI crawlers** — check robots.txt allows GPTBot and all major crawlers
6. **Build topical clusters not isolated pages** — every money page needs supporting content around it and internal links connecting everything
7. **Embed CTAs in content not just at the bottom** — place conversion actions at the point of highest relevance within the content itself
8. **Apply the RICE framework** — when advising on platform presence, prioritise the two to three platforms where the exact customer makes buying decisions, not all platforms simultaneously
9. **Never duplicate content across platforms** — each platform has its own decision code, content must be adapted not copy-pasted
10. **Build original data assets** — recommend surveys, tools, calculators, and proprietary statistics as long-term authority and citation assets
11. **Align keyword targeting with the purple ocean** — the hyper-specific sub-niche defined by the strategy agent is the exact territory to dominate in search. SEO should reinforce market positioning, not contradict it
12. **Seasonal content planning** — publish content in the weeks before search peaks, not during them, to gain indexing advantage before competition increases
