# OFFER_SKILL.md
## Offer & Lead Magnet Frameworks — Agent Reference Guide

This skill encodes the core offer and lead magnet frameworks from "How to Make Better Offers and Lead Magnets." The agent must run through these frameworks BEFORE any marketing is built. A great offer comes first. Marketing amplifies it — it cannot fix a bad one.

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM THIS SKILL SOLVES

Most businesses are commoditised. When a customer can easily compare your offer to a competitor's, you compete on price and lose. The solution is not better marketing — it is a structurally better offer that cannot be directly compared.

**The order of operations is always:**
1. Build or fix the offer (this skill)
2. Build the lead magnet (this skill)
3. Market it (MARKETING_SKILL.md)

Never skip to step 3.

---

## PART 1: THE OFFER

### The Three Offer Vectors — Pick ONE and Dominate It

You do not need all three. Pick the one most relevant to your market, execute it exceptionally, and use it to justify a premium price. Being better on even one vector removes you from commodity comparison.

**Vector 1: Speed**
- Ask: what would it take to deliver the outcome in one-third the time?
- Do not say "we can't do that." Answer the actual question — what would it take?
- If it costs more to deliver faster, raise the price to reflect it. People pay for speed.
- If the mass market won't pay, go upmarket to customers who will.
- Example: same-day or next-day service when competitors take a week

**Vector 2: Risk Reduction**
- Ask: what is the customer's biggest fear about buying this, and can we guarantee against it or insure against it?
- Mechanisms: money-back guarantee, performance guarantee, warranties, or an actual insurance policy on the outcome
- Insurance policies on customer fears are often far cheaper than expected — include the cost in your pricing
- The goal is to make buying feel safer than not buying

**Vector 3: Ease**
- Ask: how much of the customer's effort can we remove before, during, and after the sale?
- Pre-work, onboarding prep, intake simplification, white-glove setup — anything that removes friction
- Most businesses do not think this way, so customers notice and remember it when you do
- Ease is underrated as a differentiator because it is invisible until someone experiences it

### The Four Attraction Mechanisms (Wrap Around Any Offer)

Use these when you need cheaper leads or want to make an offer irresistible upfront. They work on top of whatever you currently sell.

**1. Giveaway**
- Give away the core product or a version of it
- Call every runner-up personally and offer a "second place prize" — a significant discount on the real thing
- Works especially well in home services, local businesses, and physical products

**2. Win Your Money Back**
- Customer pays, but has a chance to earn it back through performance or engagement
- Works best in service-based or results-driven businesses where the customer has to do something

**3. Decoy**
- Use a stripped-down version of your offer at a lower price to make the full offer look better by comparison
- Works best in transactional or product-based businesses

**4. Buy-Get-Free**
- Give away more free than the customer pays for, adjust the price accordingly
- Rule: the free component should feel more valuable than what they paid
- Example: instead of £1,200/year, do "buy 4 months get 8 free" at £300/month — same revenue, dramatically different perception
- Always check advertising compliance laws for your region

### Pricing Principle
A 10–20% discount changes almost no one's behaviour. An 80–90% discount on a specific component changes everything. Structure your discounts to be significant enough to actually move people — then protect your margins by applying the discount to something that costs you very little to deliver.

## PART 2: THE LEAD MAGNET

### Why Lead Magnets Beat Direct Offers at Scale

- Direct offer ads (buy now) only reach the warmest, smallest segment of your market — people already ready to buy
- Lead magnets reach further up the awareness ladder — people who have the problem but aren't ready to buy yet
- Lead magnets have lower upfront ROAS but higher overall ROAS when you extend the time horizon
- Lead magnets scale better because the total addressable audience is much larger
- If you only want to make a small amount of money, skip the lead magnet. If you want to scale, you cannot skip it.

### The Non-Negotiable Rule
Never send traffic to a page that just says "request a quote" or "book a call." That is not a lead magnet. Give people something in exchange for their contact information — something they actually want.

### The Three Lead Magnet Types

**Type 1: Reveal a Problem**
- Show the customer a problem they have but may not be fully aware of
- Format: "7 mistakes [target customer] make when [doing the thing]"
- Low cost to produce, works well in any niche
- Goal: create problem awareness and position you as the expert who knows how to solve it

**Type 2: Trial of the Solution**
- Give a genuine taste of the outcome before they pay
- This is the most powerful type when you can pull it off
- Example: "We'll get your business to the top 3 in local search for free — then call you in 7 days"
- The customer experiences the result before committing. Objections collapse.
- Works best when: you can automate delivery, the cost to deliver is low, and the perceived value is high

**Type 3: One Step of Multi-Step (The Splinter Offer)**
- Take one component of your full offer and deliver it free or at a heavy discount
- The splinter creates desire for the rest of the offer — people who get A want to buy B, C, and D
- Works in almost every business. The objection "everything I do comes together" is almost never true — there is always a step you can pull out.
- Examples: measure before fixing, colour palette before full styling consult, audit before full service, first session before full programme

### How to Build a Splinter Offer

If your full offer has components A, B, C, D at £2,000:
1. Identify the component that costs you the least to deliver (e.g. B)
2. Remove B from the main offer
3. Offer B alone for free or 80–90% off
4. Once they have B, they now want A, C, D — sell the rest at full price

The splinter is not a loss leader. It is a conversion mechanism. It gets people in the door who would not have bought the full thing cold.

### Lead Magnet Quality Rule
The more real the lead magnet is — the more it has genuine perceived cost and value — the better it will convert. Give away something other people charge for. A checklist no one else has bothered to make is worthless. A real deliverable that costs money elsewhere is gold.

## PART 3: THE OFFER–MARKETING CONNECTION

### What This Means in Practice

Before briefing the marketing agent, the offer agent must answer:
1. Which vector does this offer win on — speed, risk, or ease?
2. Is there an attraction mechanism wrapping the offer?
3. Is there a lead magnet? Which type?
4. If it's a splinter — what is the splinter and what does it lead into?

Only once these are answered should marketing copy, ads, or campaigns be built. Marketing an unclear or weak offer wastes every pound of ad spend.

### The Diagnostic Questions
If leads are expensive or volume is low, ask:
- Does the offer win on speed, risk, or ease? If not — fix the offer first.
- Is there a lead magnet? If not — add one before scaling ad spend.
- Is the lead magnet something people actually want, or just a reason to give their email?
- Is there a splinter that could lower the barrier to entry?

If conversion rate is low despite cheap leads:
- Is the lead magnet delivering on its promise?
- Is the follow-up process converting lead magnet leads into buyers?
- Does the main offer feel like a natural next step from the lead magnet?

## AGENT OPERATING PRINCIPLES

When evaluating or building any offer, the agent must:

1. **Identify the commodity trap first** — is this offer directly comparable to competitors? If yes, fix it before anything else
2. **Pick one vector** — speed, risk, or ease. Do not try to win on all three at once. Go deep on one.
3. **Choose an attraction mechanism** if the offer needs to generate leads more cheaply
4. **Always recommend a lead magnet** unless the business is explicitly only targeting the warmest buyers and has no interest in scaling
5. **Default to the splinter** when a lead magnet is needed — it is the most versatile and highest-converting type
6. **Apply the discount rule** — any discount offered must be big enough to change behaviour (80–90%), applied to a low-cost component
7. **Never build marketing until the offer is solid** — hand off to MARKETING_SKILL.md only once the offer structure is clear
